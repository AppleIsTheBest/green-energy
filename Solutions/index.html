Green Energy Practices

LED Lights

Using LED lighting can help create a green household. LED lights are brighter, use less energy, and cost less to power. As a result of the reduced energy costs, they have a lower carbon footprint creating a greener home.


Estimated Cost: $2-$10 per bulb

Learn More

ENERGYSTAR - Appliances

ENERGYSTAR is a U.S. government standard set and maintained by the U.S. Department of Energy. ENERGYSTAR products are designed to consume less energy, and can help you save on your energy bills. ENERGYSTAR compliant appliances have an ENERGYSTAR label on them.


Estimated Savings: Up to 20% of Energy Bill

Learn More

Power Off Unused Devices

Unused devices consume power when they remained powered on. Powering off unused devices including monitors, TVs, lights, and other devices can help reduce energy usage. This in turn can lower your energy bill and help create a more green home.


Estimated Savings: Dependant On Household

Learn More

Optimise Heating/Cooling

Strategising to maximise the heating/cooling systems in your home can help create a more green household by using less energy to heat/cool your home. You can use curtains, windows, and window blinds to allow/restrict sunlight to achieve this.


Estimated Savings: Dependant on Household

ENERGYSTAR - Home Owners

Electric Vehicles (EV)

Electric vehicles are becoming more common as well as more affordable. Electric vehicles are a great way to reduce emissions and may be eligible for a tax deductible of up to $7,500.
